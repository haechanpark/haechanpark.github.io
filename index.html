A little about me

Hello there! I am Hae Chan Park, an engineer who graduated from the University of California, Santa Barbara studying Electrical Engineering. Within the broad practice of Electrical Engineering, I am especially passionate about PCB design, computer architecture, FPGAs, and other applications of digital logic. However, on top of the technical aspects of engineering, I also love writing and speaking about it through documentation or personal research.

I am akin to rigorous work and advanced topics as an engineer. However, I also enjoy other extracurricular activities as I personally believe that they bring out personality. I love working out, playing tennis, and practicing the violin. Tinkering with electronics and retrocomputing are also hobbies that I invest much of my time in, especially recently.

ARTYA7 Artix-7 100T FPGA Development Board Tests

June 2023 - August 2023

Using the Digilent ARTYA7 Artix-7 100T FPGA board along with Vivado Suite and Verilog to develop basic tests for computational acceleration chips. In collaboration with the Kim Group @ UCSB.

ATmega328 Computer

May 2023 - July 2023

Rudimentary 8-bit functional computer that runs a version of Basic through the usage of two ATmega328PU chips. Utilizes PS/2 and VGA ports for input and output respectively.

IIC-1 Breadboard Processor

February 2023 - April 2023

A 4-bit breadboard processor built from various 7400 series TTL chips and other relevant components. Designed to learn more about computer architecture and digital logic design. Possesses 16 programmable addresses and can compute and store basic operations. Inspired by Ben Eater.

Waste on Wheels Robot

January 2022 - April 2022

An Arduino-based robot designed to prevent littering and environmental damage through its mobile capabilities as a trash bin. Utilizes radio and camera modules to allow for accurate movement to high concentration trash locations.
